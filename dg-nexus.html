  • Tom Baumbach: Subspace Concentration Conditions and Cone-Volumes

    Abstract: We present a discrete approach to the logarithmic Minkowski problem by defining the subspace concentration polytope, which is derived from the subspace concentration condition established by Böröczky, Lutwak, Yang, and Zhang. The close relation between the subspace concentration polytope and the basis matroid polytope is explained, allowing us to apply matroid theory. Additionally, we define the cone-volume set of a given set of outer unit normal vectors that corresponds to a polytope. The equality case between the subspace concentration polytope and the cone-volume set is characterized, and we show that the closure of the latter is a semialgebraic set. Lastly, the convex hull of the cone-volume set is fully characterized in the planar case. This talk is based on joint work with Martin Henk.
